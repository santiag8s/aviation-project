✈️ Learning to Fly

By: David Daza Salcedo

Mastering Aviation Through Simulation

🎯 My Project

For my Genius Hour project, I am learning how to fly an airplane using a flight simulator. My goal is not only to practice basic flying, but to understand how real aviation works from the inside out. Throughout this project, I am studying how pilots control an aircraft, how takeoff and landing procedures are performed, and how navigation systems guide a plane safely from one point to another.

💫 Why Aviation?

Aviation has always inspired me. There is something powerful about watching an aircraft take off, knowing that a combination of physics, engineering, and human skill makes flight possible. Using a simulator allows me to explore this world safely while still experiencing the realism of controlling an actual aircraft. Through each session, I get closer to understanding how pilots think, react, and make decisions in the air. This project is not just about flying — it's about learning discipline, precision, and the science behind one of humanity's greatest achievements.

📚 Steps Learning Plan

Step 1 — Basic Controls

Throttle, yoke, rudder, lift, drag, thrust and weight.

Understanding how each control affects the aircraft is fundamental. I learned to identify and manipulate all primary flight controls.

Step 2 — Takeoff & Flight

Runway alignment, rotation speed, climb rate and trimming.

Executing a smooth takeoff requires precision timing and understanding aircraft weight and aerodynamics.

Step 3 — Navigation & Instruments

Reading altimeter, airspeed, attitude indicator and compass.

Cockpit instruments are crucial for safe flight. I mastered instrument interpretation for VFR navigation.

Step 4 — Landing & Final Flight

Approach patterns, descent control and flare timing.

The most challenging aspect: executing a smooth, accurate landing is a true test of pilot skill.
