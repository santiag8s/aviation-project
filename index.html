Learning to Fly

By: David Daza Salcedo

My Project

For my Genius Hour project, I am learning how to fly an airplane using a flight simulator. My goal is not only to practice basic flying, but to understand how real aviation works from the inside out. Throughout this project, I am studying how pilots control an aircraft, how takeoff and landing procedures are performed, and how navigation systems guide a plane safely from one point to another. I am also learning to read essential cockpit instruments, manage altitude and airspeed, and follow realistic flight protocols used in actual aviation training. By the end of this project, I want to be able to complete a full flight—from runway to runway—using real techniques, real procedures, and real aviation knowledge.

Why Aviation?

Aviation has always inspired me. There is something powerful about watching an aircraft take off, knowing that a combination of physics, engineering, and human skill makes flight possible. Using a simulator allows me to explore this world safely while still experiencing the realism of controlling an actual aircraft. Through each session, I get closer to understanding how pilots think, react, and make decisions in the air. This project is not just about flying — it’s about learning discipline, precision, and the science behind one of humanity’s greatest achievements.

Steps Learning Plan

Step 1 — Basic Controls

Throttle, yoke, rudder, lift, drag, thrust and weight. Understanding how each control affects the aircraft.

Step 2 — Takeoff & Stable Flight

Runway alignment, rotation speed, climb rate, trimming and maintaining stable altitude and heading.

Step 3 — Navigation & Instruments

Reading altimeter, airspeed, attitude indicator, compass and VFR navigation techniques.

Step 4 — Landing & Final Flight

Approach patterns, descent control, flare timing and completing a full realistic flight.

Final Product

My final product will be a fully recorded demonstration flight where I apply everything I learned throughout the project. The video will show me performing a complete flight in a simulator — from takeoff to navigation to landing — using real aviation techniques. This recording will serve as proof of my progress and as a visual summary of my understanding of how pilots operate an aircraft in real life.
